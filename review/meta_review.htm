Title Convolutional Neural Networks on Graphs with Fast Localized Spectral Filtering
Masked Meta-Reviewer ID: Meta_Reviewer_1
Meta-Reviews:
Question 
Area chair summary: Please summarize the reasons for your decision in a couple of sentences. The paper proposes a generalized CNN on graphs by extending Henaff-Bruna-LeCun. This paper presents a promising method with theoretical analysis, with some experimental demonstration on some benchmarks (MNIST and 20 News). The reviews are overall positive, but there are some weaknesses: the presentation should be improved and self-contained, and more positive experimental results should be provided for other (real and large-scale) datasets. Still, given that this research area is relatively underexplored, most reviewers and this AC agree that the paper is worthy of publication at NIPS.

Additional comment by R1 after rebuttal:

"After reading the author feedback I am somewhat less optimistic about the paper.

The proposed algorithm is an extension of our work (Henaff-Bruna-LeCun) which indeed overcomes an important computational burden ( O(N^2) operations both at train and test time). The price to pay for a more efficient algorithm is a restriction on the class of filters, since they are all rotationally invariant. This was not even described in the original submission as far as I remember. While I believe that this restriction is worth having on high-dimensional, highly irregular graphs, the current experiments do not really test the algorithm in that regime.

Also, the connection with the previous GNN model of Scarseli et al. is an important aspect that has also been overlooked. The proposed algorithm is an important specific instance of a GNN (and the connection to GNN is not made in any prior literature I believe), but the impression is that this specific instance should be compared to the more general GNN baseline, which generalizes polynomials of the Laplacian to other nonlinear combinations of the Laplacian.

Overall, my impression is that this paper could be much improved. In its current form it is borderline, but I will not oppose acceptance if the rest of the reviewers agree."
